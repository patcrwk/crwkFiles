**Formation Architecture Summary - Open Record Platform**

**Structural Requirements:** The Open Record's formation hinges on three non-negotiable conditions that must be encoded structurally: legibility (structural quality of legislation visible to non-experts), memory (permanent, unrevised civic record), and methodological independence (assessment framework insulated from political/financial pressure). These conditions must be maintained continuously rather than produced episodically. The governing principle is methodological consistency under pressure — the one element that, if compromised once under sufficient stress, cannot be reconstructed.

**Design Implications:** This is condition-maintenance architecture, not outcome-production architecture. The platform's purpose creates structural requirements for independence that will conflict with traditional revenue models, political relationships, and optimization pressures. The formation design must anticipate and defend against moments when applying the methodology consistently becomes costly — because inconsistency, even once, destroys what cannot be rebuilt: the structural credibility that makes the other conditions possible.

**Critical Formation Note:** The architecture must distinguish between what can be lost and rebuilt (technology, donors, data) versus what cannot survive compromise (methodological consistency). This suggests governance structures that can maintain independence during funding crises, political pressure, or institutional capture attempts — the classic formation challenge of encoding public good structurally rather than rhetorically.
