**Structural Reality:**
The Open Record has achieved exceptional formation-phase clarity (purpose 8.5, awareness 8.9) and methodological rigor, but operates with catastrophic governance deficiency (2.8). This isn't gradual institutional decay — it's systematic deferral of formation conditions while scaling impact. The platform has solved the hardest problems (credible diagnostic framework, state-scale deployment) while creating maximum vulnerability in the easiest ones (legal structure, succession planning, transparency protocols). Credibility rests entirely on founder integrity rather than institutional architecture.

**Design Imperatives:**
The formation architecture must address three structural constraints: methodology governance that preserves nonpartisan credibility under political pressure, infrastructure independence that supports permanence claims, and stewardship succession that eliminates single-point-of-failure risk. The dominant force is drift — not compromise under pressure, but systematic avoidance of institutional design that would prevent future compromise. Pre-expansion governance completion is non-negotiable; scaling before stewardship architecture is complete will amplify governance debt exponentially and invite credibility attacks targeting structure, not accuracy.
